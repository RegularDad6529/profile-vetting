### @Dylanwade Connection

The 0.28 ETH payment from @dylanwade on May 28, 2023 is the largest incoming ETH transfer and warrants closer examination:

**What we know:**
- **Transaction**: Plain ETH transfer (empty input data, no smart contract interaction, no NFT transfer in the same transaction)
- **@dylanwade profile**: Level 22, 18,653 rep, 68,354 TDH, classification GOVERNMENT_NAME (real name verified). X: @dylanwadefilm, Discord: dylanwadefilm. A filmmaker and art collector on 6529.
- **No NFT transfer**: Dylanwade received 0 NFTs from kiramoto — neither direct nor through marketplace. His NFT holdings show no Kiramotosan collection pieces.
- **No reverse payment**: Kiramoto never sent any ETH back to dylanwade. This was a one-way transfer.
- **No shared counterparties**: Zero overlap between kiramoto's 3 counterparties and dylanwade's 15 counterparties.
- **No round-trip pattern**: Single one-way transfer, not circular.
- **Timing**: May 2023, 2 months after kiramoto's OpenSea sales. Dylanwade's tx was a standalone simple ETH transfer.

**Possible explanations:**
1. **Direct art purchase** — Dylanwade may have purchased a piece directly (off-marketplace) and received the NFT through a separate transaction that we can't trace, or the NFT was delivered off-chain
2. **Commission** — Dylanwade (a filmmaker) may have commissioned artwork from kiramoto
3. **Gift/Support** — Dylanwade may have sent ETH to support the artist
4. **Off-platform transaction** — The NFT may have been transferred through a marketplace we haven't checked, or delivered manually

**Assessment**: The payment appears to be a legitimate one-way transfer from an established 6529 community member (L22, GOVERNMENT_NAME, filmmaker) to kiramoto. No signs of wash trading or circular patterns. The lack of an associated NFT transfer is unusual but not suspicious — it suggests a direct relationship between the two that predates or exists outside marketplace mechanics.
